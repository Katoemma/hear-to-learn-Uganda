    • Hear to Learn Uganda

    Making education accessible for visually impaired learners Hear to Learn Uganda is a project that creates audio textbooks for primary and secondary school learners who are blind in Uganda. Our goal is to improve their access to education and promote inclusive learning. We partner with the Ministry of Education, curriculum developers, teachers, publishers, and local organizations to produce high-quality audio textbooks that are relevant to the Ugandan context. We also provide a website where learners, teachers, and parents can access these audio textbooks and other resources. Join us in our mission to make education accessible for all!

    About Us

    Hear to Learn Uganda is a project that was founded in 2022 by a group of passionate individuals who wanted to make a difference in the lives of visually impaired learners in Uganda. We believe that education is a human right and that everyone deserves equal opportunities to learn and grow. Our vision is a world where visually impaired learners can access quality education without barriers. Our mission is to create audio textbooks that are relevant, engaging, and accessible for visually impaired learners in Uganda. We work with a team of dedicated staff members, volunteers, partners, and donors who share our values and goals. We are always looking for new ways to improve our project and expand our reach

    Impact

    Hear to Learn Uganda has a positive impact on the lives of visually impaired learners and their communities. Some of the benefits of our project are: Improved access to education: Our audio textbooks enable visually impaired learners to access the same curriculum content as their sighted peers, without relying on braille or other costly materials. Enhanced learning outcomes: Our audio textbooks improve the comprehension, retention, and performance of visually impaired learners, as they can listen and learn at their own pace and convenience. Increased confidence and self-esteem: Our audio textbooks empower visually impaired learners to participate more actively in class discussions, assignments, and exams, boosting their confidence and self-esteem. Reduced stigma and discrimination: Our audio textbooks raise awareness and understanding of visual impairment among teachers, parents, and society at large, reducing stigma and discrimination against visually impaired learners.
